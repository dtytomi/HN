  3. Qualitative Research Approach


At HNC qualitative research helps organizations in decision making and deriving deeper insights using techniques in which information is obtained from a relatively small group of people regarding their thoughts or opinions about services or products rather than from statistical inferences. We do this using group discussion and direct face to face interviews using discussion guides.

Our methods include:

  • Immersion
  • IDIs
  • FGDs
  • Ethnography
  • Social listening
  • Mystery observation

Our qualitative research processes involves the following:

  • Advisory
  • Setup / planning
  • Designs
  • Execution
  • Analysis and reporting
  • Presentations

Advisory

We offer advisory services for startups or already existing organizations, helping to formulate strategies that will grow and improve the product or service in a generally competitive market with other existing players. The process trails from client engagement to kick off meetings, proposals and project commissioning.

Setup/planning

Our setup for the qualitative research involves the planning phase where discussion guide designs are usually specific to the objectives, questions, insights and business outcomes that the clients want answered in relation to their service or product or have in view as well as the sampling procedures and the timelines for deliverables.

Designs

In designing the survey for the set study, we at HNC ensure that there is a deep understanding of the project by teaming with the clients to ensure that the output of the study is reliable. This is reflected in the sharing and review of the discussion guides to capture the objectives in addition to the recruitment of team using relevant criteria, selection of the appropriate venue considering the possible sensitivity of the project, gadget control and testing (such as midget, screens, monitors, microphones and video camera)recording test to ascertain reliability.

Execution

In qualitative approach project execution covers moderation and facilitation for each discussion or interaction, the various types of interviews conducted, quality control to ensure that the information is collected appropriately (not biased or coerced) and rightly done to detect frauds while ensuring that all interviews and discussions are properly guided with accurate and valuable information. Also real time updates, feed backs and transfer of recordings are done correctly. This is execution is based on experience of the team, the quality of work being delivered, training of the working team, certification of the participants.

Analysis and reporting

Information gathered from the field are transcribed (could also involve translations) carefully ensuring that summaries that lose track of important details are not done and relevant information captured during the interview processes are retrieved accurately. The information is analyzed and reporting based on the objectives and outcomes indicated by the client while making certain only important and relevant data are presented and discussed.

Presentations

Presentations of the findings and outcomes with relevant insights that address the objectives and other significant information that will be useful for clients decision making process. In addition feasible recommendations are made after draft submissions and then final reports delivered following client reviews.
