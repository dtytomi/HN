Using the quantitative research methodology HNC applies techniques applicable in this area to investigate products or services using different data collection processes such as structured customer surveys and questionnaires to generate solid facts through reliable statistics for informed decision and insights into market situation peculiar to the clients. This is done by means of CATI, CAPI, OUTCOME and SMS.

The quantitative further broken down into:

  • Advisory
  • Setup / planning
  • Designs
  • Execution
  • Analysis and reporting
  • Presentations

Advisory

We offer advisory services for startups or already existing organizations, helping to formulate strategies that will grow and improve the business in a generally competitive market with other existing players. The process trails from client engagement to kick off meetings, proposals and project commissioning.

Setup/planning

Our setup for the quantitative research involves the planning phase where questionnaire designs are usually specific to the objectives, questions, insights and business outcomes that the clients want answered or have in view as well as the sampling procedures and the timelines for deliverables.

Designs

In designing the survey for the set study, we at HNC ensure that there is a deep understanding of the project by teaming with the clients to ensure that the output of the study is reliable. This is reflected in the sharing and review of questionnaires to capture the objectives in addition to the team selection process, testing of the scripts, a pilot survey to ensure that itâ€™s representative and feasible. This stage covers all required applications, software and the mobile devices employed for the projects.

Execution

Project execution is all encompassing involving, fieldwork, Data capturing, GPS monitoring for the interviewers, quality control to ensure that the data collected is appropriate and rightly done to detect frauds and any unmet quota. Also real time updates, data cleaning, live feed back and results. This is execution is based on experience of the team, the quality of work being delivered, training of the working team, certification of the participants.

Analysis and reporting

Data from the field are managed and analysed using standard procedures. Modelling and formatting are done ensuring that results are presented on the basis of objectives and outcomes indicated by the client while making certain only important and relevant data are presented in tables and charts discussed.

Presentations

Presentations of the findings and outcomes with relevant insights and other information that address the objectives alongside feasible recommendations are made to the client after draft submissions and then final reports following reviews.
